Negative Differential Thermal Conductance by Photonic Heat Transport

Internship project at Quantum Research Center, TII | April 2022 - July 2022

The negative differential thermal conductance (NDTC) provides the key mechanism for realizing thermal transistors. This exotic effect has been the object of an extensive theoretical investigation, but the implementation is still limited to a few specific physical systems. In my project supervised by Prof. Luigi Amico, we devise a general mechanism to engineer NDTC and demostrate how superconducting-normally conducting phase transistion can be exploited to gain NDTC via heat transport in photonic regime. Our paper had been submitted to Physical Review B where it has recently recieved lauding comments from all the reviewers and is in the final editing stage.

Turbulence in Exciton Polariton Condensates

Charpak Lab Scholar at PhLAM, CNRS| May 2022 - July 2022

The aim of the project was to perform a time resolved study of superfluid of exciton-polariton condensate flowing against a defect acting as an obstacle to study vortex shedding and eventually tubulence. Under the guidance of CNRS Researcher Alberto Amo this was my first experimental project and I got the oppurtunity to build up everything from the very scratch, starting from optical allignments, electronics, calibrating the optomechanics and cryogenics, characterizing the dispersion curves and condensation thresholds using Fourier space spectroscopy to finally probing the desired phenomena. The internship was funded by the Charpak Lab Scholarship awarded to 30 students by the French Embassy in India.

Ongoing Master's Thesis Project

Theoretical and Experimental study of nonequilibrium physics in steady state exciton polariton condensates

Advisor: Prof. Vishwanath Shukla and Prof. Sajal Dhara, Department of Physics, IIT Kharagpur | Aug 2022 - March 2023

Starting with developing a robust code to simulate coherent and incoherent condensation of exciton polaritons in a microcavity, we have headed on to theoretically model nonequilbrium physics like KPZ or BKT phenomena in 2D polariton condensates under different lattice backgrounds like anisotropy or disorder, parallelly designing the experiments to characterize these in steady states i.e without time resolution. The theoretical quantum fluidic parts of the project are being advised by Prof Vishwanath Shukla while the experimental realization shall be done under the supervision of Prof Sajal Dhara
In the right, you see a simulation of an incoherent condensation of polaritons using a Gaussian pump in presence of disorder, tuned to produce Airy like waves slowly stabilizing to a steady circular fringes and a nearly constant total number of polaritons. Also we see interference of these Airy waves with the reflected waves from boundaries in the later stages of simulation which eventually produces turbulent behaviour.

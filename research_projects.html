Trapped Ion Quantum Simulation

Visiting Researcher at Pagano Lab, Rice University | Houston, USA | Oct 2023 - Present

Currently working on two projects parallel while more aligned for further. We use Yb171+ ions for quantum simulation setup which is Dopler cooled by 369.5nm transition. In previous experiments, it has been observed that far more optimal cooling times and bandwidth can be achieved with higher power, low noise Doppler beams. But buying such lasers in the power range of 30-70mW is expensive, and this is where injection-locking comes in. This commonly used technique in AMO-labs uses a low power but low phase and amplitude noise Master Laser , a 369.5nm Moglabs ECLDL in our case, to lock the frequency and phase of high power but also noisy and broader band Slave Laser, a 372nm Nichia's laser diode.

This is done by controlling the current and temperature of the slave laser diode. In this project, I designed a compact module packing the diode and Thermoelectric coolers diffused with Nitrogen to avoid dew. A PID-based analog circuit to control the TECs is designed to keep the temperature stable while the current is controlled by Thorlabs LDC202C. To adjust for the ambient temperature drifts in the lab, injection lock efficiency is digitally processed from a Fabry-Perot cavity's spectra and fed back to the current controller to change the setpoint accordingly.

Negative Differential Thermal Conductance by Photonic Heat Transport

Internship project at Quantum Research Center, TII | Abu Dhabi, UAE | Aug 2021 - Feb 2022

The negative differential thermal conductance (NDTC) provides the key mechanism for realizing thermal transistors. This exotic effect has been the object of an extensive theoretical investigation, but the implementation is still limited to a few specific physical systems. In my project supervised by Prof. Luigi Amico, we devise a general mechanism to engineer NDTC and demostrate how superconducting to normally conducting phase transition can be exploited to gain NDTC via heat transport in photonic regime, including the effects of electron-phonon coupling while modeling. Our paper was accepted by Physical Review B

Turbulence in Exciton Polariton Condensates

Charpak Lab Scholar | PhLAM, CNRS | Lille, France | May 2022 - July 2022

The aim of the project was to perform a time resolved study of superfluid of exciton-polariton condensate flowing against a defect acting as an obstacle to study vortex shedding and eventually turbulence. Under the guidance of CNRS Researcher Alberto Amo I got the oppurtunity to build up my first experiment, starting from optical allignments, electronics, setting up the optomechanics and cryogenics, characterizing the dispersion curves and condensation thresholds using Fourier space spectroscopy to finally probe the desired phenomena. The 2-month internship was funded by the Charpak Lab Scholarship awarded to 30 students by the French Embassy in India.

Master's Thesis Project

Study of nonequilibrium physics in steady state exciton-polariton condensates

Advisor: Prof. Vishwanath Shukla and Prof. Sajal Dhara, Department of Physics, IIT Kharagpur | Aug 2022 - March 2023

Having developed a robust code to simulate coherent and incoherent condensation of exciton-polaritons in a microcavity, we have headed on to theoretically model nonequilbrium physics like turbulence and KPZ in the condensate. We also plane to setup the experiments to characterize these in steady states i.e without using time resolution. The theoretical quantum fluidic parts of the project are being advised by Prof. Vishwanath Shukla while the experimental realization shall be done under the supervision of Prof. Sajal Dhara

In the right, you see our simulation of an incoherent condensation of polaritons using a Gaussian pump with added noise in its intensity. Due to the collison of radially outwards waves and the reflected waves quantized vortices are excited, which at a threshold of nonlinearity in the system, interact to form larger clusters of vortices, thereby tranferring incompressible kinetic energy from smaller to larger length scales. This is seen as the Kolmogorov's -5/3 power law dependency in the spectrum of the incompressible kinetic energy. Other details on the theory, numerical scheme and analysis can be found in the Thesis.

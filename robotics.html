After some thorough practice, I joined the research group Autonomous Ground Vehicles(AGV) as an Embedded and Controls Team Member. For the next 1.5 years, before the physics curriculum showed its full glory, I have had the pleasure of being a part of a fleet of lucrative robotics projects and a team of enthusiastic roboticists that any undergrad would cherish. What follows is a glimpse of work I did with my fellow members at AGV.

Autonav Challenge at the International Ground Vehicle Competition 2019

Eklavya 7.0 | June 2019

We built our bot, named Eklavya 7.0, in the summer break of 1st year, for the Autonav Challenge, IGVC 2019, held in Michigan, USA. The problem statement was to build a fully autonomous robot capable of planning its path through white lanes drawn over grass, avoiding obstacles and also navigating by GPS in an unmarked land. As an Embedded and Controls team member I set up the controls module and integrated all the sensors, automation softwares and controls via the Robot Operating System or ROS architecture. Our team bagged the 2nd prize for the 2nd consecutive time that year.

Control Systems and Path Planning

July 2019 - July 2020

Our team was collectively working to automate the Mahindra E2O car at the highest levels of autonomy possible. So consider the car driving itself in a certain environment with lanes and other obstacles, based on which the perception and planning modules have generated an intermediate path to be followed. Now the job of controls module is send signals to the steering wheel, accelerator and brakes at every timestep for the care to optimally trace the path generated. We achieved doing this with various algorithms of different complexity and efficiency. Amongst the algorithms, Pure Pursuit and Stanley were kinematic controllers while the Linear Quadratic Regulator(LQR) was an optimal dynamic controller. We further successfully built the iterative-LQR that could generate the path as well as the control signals and made significant progress in Gaussian Process iterative-Linear Quadratic Gaussian that additionally also filters sensor noise and autocorrects modelling errors on the go(usefull when dynamics change, like in the case of reduced pressure in tyres or entering a muddy road).
